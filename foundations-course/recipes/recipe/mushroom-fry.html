Mushroom Fry

Description

Mushroom fry is a delicious and quick dish that showcases the earthy flavors of mushrooms, typically sautéed with aromatic spices and herbs. This dish can be served as a side or a main course, making it a versatile option for any meal. The mushrooms are often paired with onions, garlic, and spices like cumin and coriander to enhance their natural taste. With its savory profile and appealing texture, mushroom fry is a favorite among both vegetarians and mushroom lovers.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams of mushrooms (button or cremini)
  • 1 medium onion (sliced)
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 green chili (slit, optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable or olive oil)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Clean the mushrooms with a damp cloth and slice them into thick pieces.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add the sliced onions and green chili, sautéing until the onions are translucent.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the sliced mushrooms and stir well, cooking until they release their moisture.
  6. Sprinkle in the turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Mix thoroughly and cook for another 5-7 minutes until the mushrooms are tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving hot.
